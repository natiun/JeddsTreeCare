Emeralad Ash Borer

The Emeralad Ash Borer is a exotic beetle that was recently discovered in 2002 near southeastern Michigan. The adult beetles feed on ash foliage but cause small damage. Emerald ash borer probably arrived in the United States on solid wood packing material carried in cargo ships or airplanes originating in its native Asia.

Since the Emeralad Ash Borer has been discovered

The emeralad ash borer has killed millions of ash trees in the North American region. These bugs have costed municipalities, nursery operators, property owners, and forest products industries multi-millions of dollars

Where the emerald ash broer has been found?

The emerald ash borer has been detected in places such as Larimer County, CO, Crittenden County, KY, Cumberland, ME, Windham County, VT, Wilkes County, NC, and Saunders County, NE.
